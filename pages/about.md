I was born and raised in Rock Springs, Wyoming, a town of about ~22,000 people (5th largest town in the state!). In a past life, I would've been a development economist, hoping to understand the mechanisms of poverty in the global South and the potential tools we can use to alleviate poverty. Having been sidetracked immediately in my undergrad by the honors freshman math sequence at Columbia, I changed my sights onto mathematics and have rarely looked back since. 

As a rural student, I am deeply invested in issues that pertain to the accessibility of higher education to all students, especially across income levels. I have been involved with the Rural Recruitment Committee at Columbia, helping with outreach efforts for admitted and interested students across the country, and am hoping to continue that outreach work in my time at UMN.
